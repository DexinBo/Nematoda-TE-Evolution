S8_Fig.pdf
<div><p>(A) Sequencing of small RNAs following treatment with 200 mM sodium periodate compared to control samples shows that <i>C</i>. <i>elegans</i> 21U-RNAs are specifically protected against oxidation whilst <i>T</i>. <i>spiralis</i> 23&#8211;25 nt siRNAs are lost following oxidation. The peak at 28&#8211;30 nt in <i>T</i>. <i>spiralis</i> reflects two abundant ribosomal RNA sequences as shown by its loss upon collapsing the sequence data to unique sequences (far right hand panel). (B, C) Cluster analysis across the genome shows that regions with high density of piRNAs found in <i>C</i>. <i>elegans</i> and <i>D</i>. <i>melanogaster</i> are not found for <i>T</i>. <i>spiralis</i> 23&#8211;25 nt siRNAs. (B) Shows genome-wide distribution of <i>T</i>. <i>spiralis</i> 23&#8211;25 nt siRNAs, <i>C</i>. <i>elegans</i> piRNAs, and <i>D</i>. <i>melanogaster</i> piRNAs. Reads are binned in 100 kb windows across the genome and coloured by contigs or chromosomes according to the genome assembly, with the contigs or chromosomes sorted in order of the total number of small RNAs mapping to them. (C) Shows the cumulative fraction of sequences in (B) that are found in regions with greater than or equal to the density indicated on the <i>x</i>-axis. <i>C</i>. <i>elegans</i> and <i>D</i>. <i>melanogaster</i> both have more sequences mapping to higher density regions than <i>T</i>. <i>spiralis</i> does.</p>
</div>
